# How to Prepare for Your Fantasy Football Draft

<div class="subtitle">A practical preseason checklist before draft day</div>

<div class="author">Written by Kevin Tian</div>

## Introduction

Draft day is one of the best parts of fantasy football. It is also where a lot of managers start making life harder for themselves before Week 1 even kicks off.

Not because they picked one wrong player. That happens to everyone. The real problem is showing up a little too loose: not knowing the scoring settings, missing a positional run, forgetting the draft order, or panic-picking from Sleeper's default rankings with 12 seconds left on the clock.

You do not need to predict the whole season in August. You just need enough of a plan that you are not drafting like the room is happening to you.

## Confirm Your League Settings

Before looking at rankings, check your league settings. This sounds obvious, but it changes everything.

The big ones:

- Full PPR, half PPR, or standard scoring
- One QB or superflex
- Number of flex spots
- Tight end premium
- Bench size
- IR spots
- Playoff teams
- Trade deadline
- Waiver format

A player who is valuable in one format can be much less exciting in another. Superflex pushes quarterbacks up. Tight end premium changes the middle rounds. Deeper benches make upside stashes more useful. Short benches make every roster spot matter.

If you skip this step, you might be drafting for a league that does not exist.

## Check Your Draft Slot

Once the draft order is set, mock from your actual pick a few times.

Mock drafting from the 1.01 does not help much if you are picking 10th. Your decisions will feel completely different. Early picks usually mean long waits between turns. Middle picks can be more balanced. Later picks force you to think in pairs because you are often drafting near the turn.

You do not need to treat mock drafts like gospel. The point is to get familiar with the pockets of the draft. Which running backs are usually there in round three? When do the tight ends disappear? What does your team look like if you start WR-WR instead of RB-WR?

If you want a deeper look at draft slot value, I also wrote about whether [draft pick really matters](/posts/draft).

## Build Tiers, Not Just Rankings

Rankings are useful. Tiers are what actually keep you calm.

If five wide receivers are basically the same to you, put them in the same tier. If there is a big drop after those five, that is the part that matters.

Tiers help you avoid panic. If there are still several players you like at wide receiver, maybe you can wait and grab a running back. If your last tight end in a tier is about to go, maybe it is time to move.

This is also how you avoid becoming a passenger on the default draft board. Sleeper's rankings are useful, but your league settings and preferences should matter more than the order on the screen.

## Use Sleeper's Queue

Sleeper's queue is one of the easiest ways to avoid a bad pick.

Before the draft starts, add players you like at different positions. During the draft, keep updating it as players come off the board. It helps when picks move quickly, when you are drafting from your phone, or when the chat starts distracting everyone.

The queue is especially useful near the turn. If you are picking 11th or 12th in a 12-team league, you may have two picks close together and then a long wait. Queueing a few combinations ahead of time can keep you from rushing the second pick.

It also protects you if your connection drops or the app lags. Nobody wants to explain an accidental kicker pick in round seven.

## Plan for Positional Runs

Every fantasy draft has at least one run where the room suddenly decides it needs the same position.

Quarterbacks start flying off the board. Tight ends disappear. Running backs get thin. Someone takes a rookie, and three more rookies go right after.

The key is not to panic just because a run is happening. Ask yourself:

- Is this position actually drying up?
- Is there still value in another tier?
- Am I filling a need or chasing the room?
- Will this pick still make sense in two rounds?

Sometimes you should join the run. Sometimes the best move is letting everyone else chase while you take the value they are ignoring.

## Think About Roster Construction

A good draft is not just a list of good players. It is a roster that fits together.

Pay attention to how your team is forming:

- Do you have enough reliable starters?
- Are you taking enough upside on the bench?
- Did you wait too long at running back?
- Did you spend early capital on quarterback or tight end?
- Are you drafting too many players with the same bye week?

Do not obsess over bye weeks, especially early. Good players are more important. But if your entire bench disappears in the same week, that is worth noticing before you click draft.

## Know Your League Mates

This is the part rankings cannot tell you.

Every league has patterns. Someone always takes a quarterback early. Someone loves rookies. Someone drafts half their favorite NFL team. Someone waits forever on running back. Someone takes a defense way too soon and makes everyone else uncomfortable for a minute.

Use that information. If two managers before your next pick both need tight ends, do not assume your guy is coming back. If your league always lets quarterbacks fall, you might not need to force one early.

Fantasy football is not drafted in a vacuum. You are drafting against people, not rankings.

## Save the Receipts

One of the best parts of fantasy football is looking back later and seeing which draft takes aged well.

The team that "won the draft" might miss the playoffs. The manager everyone laughed at might have nailed three breakout picks. A bad draft can be saved by waivers. A great draft can be ruined by injuries.

Draft night is only the first chapter. Once the season plays out, [ffwrapped](https://ffwrapped.com) can help you look back at your Sleeper league and see which teams actually got value, which managers overcame bad luck, and which storylines mattered most.

## Conclusion

Preparing for your fantasy football draft does not have to be complicated.

Check your settings. Know your format. Mock from your draft slot. Build tiers. Use the queue. Stay calm during positional runs. Draft a roster that makes sense.

You will still miss on players. Everyone does. But a little preparation makes it much easier to avoid the preventable mistakes and actually enjoy draft night.
