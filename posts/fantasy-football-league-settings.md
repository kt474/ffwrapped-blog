# Fantasy Football League Settings Guide

Fantasy football league settings decide how the whole season feels. Scoring format changes player value. Waiver rules decide who gets breakout players. Playoff and tiebreaker rules shape what kind of teams get rewarded.

If you are setting up a fantasy football league or cleaning up your commissioner rules before draft day, start here.

## Start With the Commissioner Checklist

[Fantasy Football League Rules to Decide Before Draft Day](/posts/league-rules-before-draft) is the best starting point. It covers the settings every league should settle before the draft:

- Scoring format
- Roster settings
- Waiver rules
- Trade rules
- Playoff format
- Tiebreakers
- Keeper rules
- Commissioner powers
- Payouts and punishments

The goal is simple: make the important decisions before anyone knows which rule helps their roster.

## Choose a Scoring Format

[Half-PPR vs Full-PPR: Which Fantasy Football Scoring Format Is Better?](/posts/half-ppr-vs-full-ppr) compares the most common reception scoring formats.

Half-PPR is usually the best default for new leagues because it rewards receptions without letting short catches dominate scoring. Full-PPR can work well for deeper leagues, especially if you want more usable players and higher weekly scores.

Whatever format you choose, make sure everyone knows before the draft. Draft strategy changes when receptions, bonuses, tight end premium, or superflex settings are involved.

## Pick a Waiver System

[FAAB vs Rolling Waivers](/posts/faab-vs-rolling-waivers) breaks down the two most common waiver systems.

For active leagues, FAAB is usually better because every manager gets a chance at every player. Rolling waivers are simpler and can work for casual leagues, but they often reward patience more than decision-making.

My default recommendation:

- $100 FAAB budget
- Blind bids
- $0 bids allowed
- Waivers after games begin
- Free agency after waivers clear

## Check Your Settings After the Season

Good settings are easier to evaluate once the season is over. Did the best teams make the playoffs? Did waiver activity matter? Did tiebreakers reward total performance or one lucky head-to-head result?

[ffwrapped](https://ffwrapped.com) helps Sleeper and ESPN leagues review the season with league recaps, standings context, and manager storylines. Use it after the season to see whether your settings created the kind of league you wanted.
