# Fantasy Football League Analytics Guide

Fantasy football standings tell you who won each matchup. They do not always tell you who had the best team, who got lucky, who ran into the worst schedule, or which storylines actually defined the season.

League analytics add context. They help explain why a team won, why a good roster missed the playoffs, and which managers were better or worse than their record.

If you want a tool that does this for your own league, try the [ffwrapped fantasy football league analyzer](https://ffwrapped.com).

## Start With Schedule Luck

[The Unluckiest Team in Your Fantasy Football League](/posts/unluckiest-team) explains how to look past standings.

Useful metrics include:

- Points scored
- Points against
- All-play record
- Expected wins
- Close losses
- Playoff misses despite strong scoring

A manager can make good decisions and still lose because they faced everyone else's best weeks. A different manager can sneak into the playoffs with a soft schedule. Both stories are more interesting than the standings alone.

## Compare Draft Results to League Outcomes

[Does draft pick really matter?](/posts/draft) uses real 2024 Sleeper league data to study championship teams by draft slot and first-round player.

This kind of analysis helps answer questions every league argues about:

- Did early draft slots have an advantage?
- Which first-round picks showed up most often on winning rosters?
- Did running backs, wide receivers, or quarterbacks lead more champions?
- How much did injuries change the season?

The best fantasy football analytics usually connect roster decisions to final outcomes.

## Use League Data for Your Own Analysis

[How to use the Sleeper API](/posts/tutorial) walks through pulling public league data and building a simple visualization.

If you want to build your own analysis, Sleeper's public API gives you access to league, roster, matchup, and user data without authentication. That makes it possible to build charts for points scored, manager records, weekly results, and league history.
