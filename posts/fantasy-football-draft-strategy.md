# Fantasy Football Draft Strategy Guide

Fantasy football draft strategy starts before you are on the clock. The best managers know their scoring settings, draft slot, roster requirements, and league tendencies before draft night turns into a countdown timer.

This guide collects the ffwrapped draft strategy articles that help you prepare without pretending anyone can perfectly predict the season in August.

## Prepare Before Draft Night

[How to Prepare for Your Fantasy Football Draft](/posts/prepare-for-sleeper-draft) is the main preseason checklist.

It covers:

- Confirming scoring and roster settings
- Mocking from your actual draft slot
- Building tiers instead of blindly following rankings
- Using your platform's draft queue
- Handling positional runs
- Thinking through roster construction
- Learning your league mates' habits

The goal is not to draft a perfect team. It is to avoid preventable mistakes and make decisions that fit your actual league.

## Understand Draft Slot Value

[Does draft pick really matter?](/posts/draft) uses 2024 Sleeper league data to look at where championship teams drafted.

Draft position matters, but it is not destiny. Later picks had some advantages in the sample because of which players were available around the turn, while early picks were hurt by injuries and underperformance from expected elite players.

That kind of review is useful because it separates draft theory from what actually happened in real leagues.

## Check the Rules Before Rankings

Draft advice depends on settings. A player can be more valuable in full-PPR, less valuable in standard scoring, or dramatically more important in superflex.

Before using any rankings, read:

- [Half-PPR vs Full-PPR: Which Fantasy Football Scoring Format Is Better?](/posts/half-ppr-vs-full-ppr)
- [Fantasy Football League Rules to Decide Before Draft Day](/posts/league-rules-before-draft)

If the league settings are unusual, your draft plan should be unusual too.

## Review the Draft After the Season

Draft grades are fun in August, but the real answers come after the season. Which early picks actually returned value? Which teams recovered through waivers? Which managers were good, lucky, unlucky, or all three?

[ffwrapped](https://ffwrapped.com) helps Sleeper and ESPN leagues turn the season into a recap so you can see which draft takes aged well once the games were actually played.
