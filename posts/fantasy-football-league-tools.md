# Fantasy Football League Tools and API Guide

Fantasy football league tools are most useful when they turn platform data into a story people can actually use. League visualizations, season recaps, draft reviews, manager awards, and schedule luck analysis all help explain what happened beyond the standings.

This page collects ffwrapped guides for fantasy football league analysis, including Sleeper API projects and recap ideas for Sleeper and ESPN leagues.

## Build With the Sleeper API

[How to use the Sleeper API](/posts/tutorial) is the technical starting point.

The tutorial shows how to:

- Fetch public Sleeper league data
- Work with league users and rosters
- Pull matchup information
- Build a points visualization
- Turn raw league data into something easier to understand

If you are a developer, this is the bridge from "Sleeper has an API" to "I can actually make something with my league data."

## Prepare for Draft Night

[How to Prepare for Your Fantasy Football Draft](/posts/prepare-for-sleeper-draft) includes practical advice around checking league settings, using draft queues, and avoiding default-rankings panic before draft night.

Most fantasy platforms make it easy to draft quickly, but that also means it is easy to get pulled by the default board if you do not have a plan.

## Analyze a League After the Season

The most fun fantasy football tools are often the ones you use after the season:

- League recaps
- Schedule luck
- Standings context
- Draft pick analysis
- Manager awards
- Points scored and points against charts

[The Unluckiest Team in Your Fantasy Football League](/posts/unluckiest-team) and [Does draft pick really matter?](/posts/draft) show the kind of analysis that becomes possible when you look beyond wins and losses.

## Try ffwrapped for League Recaps

[ffwrapped](https://ffwrapped.com) turns Sleeper and ESPN league data into fantasy football recaps and league insights without requiring you to write code.

Use it when you want the story of your league: the lucky and unlucky teams, the managers who overperformed, and the season recap your league chat will actually care about.
